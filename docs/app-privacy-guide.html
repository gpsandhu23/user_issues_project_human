App Privacy Guide (Internal Reference)

Purpose: This document provides step-by-step answers for the App Store Connect App Privacy questionnaire. Use this as a reference when submitting the app or updating privacy settings.

Location: App Store Connect → Your App → App Privacy

Important: This is an internal reference document based on the actual data collection practices of the Gainz app (as of January 1, 2026). Always review the questionnaire carefully and update answers if the app's data practices change.

Section 4: Is Data Linked to the User?

Answer: YES, for ALL data types
All data collected by the app is linked to user accounts (via Apple Sign-In identifier). Users can view their nutrition and workout history, and all data is associated with their account.

Section 5: Is Data Used to Track the User?

Answer: NO
We do not use data for tracking purposes. Specifically:
  • No cross-app or cross-website tracking
  • No advertising tracking or behavioral profiling
  • No data sold to data brokers
  • Analytics are limited to in-app feature usage (not user behavior tracking for advertising)

Section 6: Do You or Your Third-Party Partners Collect Data from This App to Use for Their Own Purposes?

Answer: YES
We share user data with third-party services for app functionality:
  • OpenAI: Processes user inputs (food descriptions, images, workout inputs, mental health queries) for AI analysis
  • LangSmith: Receives usage metadata for analytics
  • Apple: Verifies In-App Purchase receipts

Third-Party Services to Disclose

Service Data Shared Purpose
OpenAI Food descriptions (text + images), workout inputs, mental health queries User inputs are processed by OpenAI's AI models for nutrition analysis, workout generation, and mental health features
LangSmith Usage metadata (request timestamps, feature types, user ID, performance metrics) Analytics and monitoring of AI service performance
Apple In-App Purchase receipts Purchase verification and fraud prevention

Key Points to Remember

  • Be Honest: Apple can reject apps for inaccurate privacy disclosures. Always reflect actual data practices.
  • Update When Changing: If you add new features or change data collection, update the App Privacy settings in App Store Connect.
  • Test Your Privacy Policy URL: Make sure it's live and accessible before submitting.
  • Third-Party AI Disclosure: Explicitly mention OpenAI in the third-party services section (required by Apple's Nov 2025 guidelines).
  • Images Are NOT Stored: While you collect photos (for nutrition analysis), clarify in your privacy policy that images are processed but not stored.
